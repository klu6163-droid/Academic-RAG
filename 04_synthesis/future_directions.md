## FD1: Machine Learning-Guided Design of Self-Healing Polyurethanes

### 1. Core Scientific Hypothesis
ML models trained on structure-property data can predict optimal hard/soft segment ratios for maximizing both toughness and self-healing efficiency.

### 2. Literature Evidence
Multiple TPU papers with systematic composition variation; ML models for polymer properties emerging.

### 3. Current Research Gap
No ML-guided optimization of self-healing TPU reported.

### 4. Innovation Analysis
Combines computational design with experimental validation for accelerated materials discovery.

### 5. Scientific Value
Could reduce development time from years to months.

### 6. Technical Feasibility
Requires synthesis capability, mechanical testing, and ML expertise.

### 7. Possible Experimental Design
1) Collect structure-property data from literature; 2) Train GNN model; 3) Predict optimal compositions; 4) Synthesize and validate.

### 8. Key Characterization Methods
Tensile testing, healing efficiency measurement, SAXS for morphology.

### 9. Expected Results
Identified composition window with >90% healing efficiency and >30 MJ/m3 toughness.

### 10. Potential Risks
Model may not generalize beyond training data.

### 11. Alternative Approaches
Bayesian optimization with smaller experimental campaigns.

### 12. Output
High-impact paper + database for community.

## FD2: In Situ SAXS/DMA Coupling for Real-Time Microphase Separation Dynamics

### 1. Core Scientific Hypothesis
Coupling in situ SAXS with simultaneous DMA measurements can reveal the kinetic coupling between microphase separation and mechanical property development.

### 2. Literature Evidence
SAXS and DMA individually well-used; coupling is rare but technically feasible.

### 3. Current Research Gap
Real-time structure-property evolution during processing is largely unknown.

### 4. Innovation Analysis
First systematic in situ study of structure-property coupling in dynamic polymer networks.

### 5. Scientific Value
Fundamental understanding of processing-structure-property relationships.

### 6. Technical Feasibility
Requires synchrotron access and custom DMA setup.

### 7. Possible Experimental Design
1) Design temperature-controlled DMA cell for SAXS; 2) Study TPU crystallization kinetics; 3) Correlate SAXS invariant with modulus.

### 8. Key Characterization Methods
SAXS, DMA, DSC, WAXD.

### 9. Expected Results
Quantitative relationship between phase separation kinetics and mechanical property development.

### 10. Potential Risks
Beam damage, limited time resolution.

### 11. Alternative Approaches
Lab-scale SAXS with slower heating rates.

### 12. Output
Methodology paper + fundamental study.
