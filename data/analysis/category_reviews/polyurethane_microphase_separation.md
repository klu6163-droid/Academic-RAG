## 1. Research Background

Polyurethane (PU) is a segmented block copolymer consisting of hard segments (diisocyanate + chain extender) and soft segments (polyol). The thermodynamic incompatibility between hard and soft segments drives microphase separation, forming hard domains dispersed in a continuous soft matrix. This morphology directly governs mechanical properties, shape memory behavior, and biocompatibility.

- **Conclusion**: In conclusion, this study presents an ultra-strong and tough bio-based polyurethane elastomer with multiple hydrogen bonds.
